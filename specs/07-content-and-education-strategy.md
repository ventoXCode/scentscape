# Content & Fragrance Education Strategy

## Job to Be Done

ScentScape's competitive moat is being the place where fragrance beginners become confident enthusiasts. Content and education — woven into every surface of the product — transforms ScentScape from "a quiz site" into "the fragrance companion." Users should learn something every time they visit, without ever feeling lectured.

## Current State

- Zero educational content on the platform
- Product detail pages list notes and accords without explaining what they mean
- No glossary, guides, or learning resources
- No blog or editorial section
- No contextual help or tooltips for fragrance terminology
- Fragrance families are presented without explanation
- The quiz doesn't teach — it only categorizes
- No content marketing or SEO-driven content strategy

## Desired Outcomes

### Contextual Education (In-Product)
- Tooltips on fragrance terms everywhere they appear (sillage, projection, EDC vs EDP, etc.)
- "What is [note]?" expandable cards on product pages — brief, sensory descriptions of individual notes
- Quiz steps should include brief context: "Fragrance families are like genres in music..."
- Scent pyramid should educate: "Top notes are what you smell first — they fade in 15-30 minutes"
- Performance ratings should explain what they mean in practical terms

### Standalone Educational Content
- "Fragrance 101" guide: comprehensive but approachable intro to fragrance
- "How to Find Your Signature Scent" — a longer-form version of what the quiz does
- "How to Apply Fragrance" — practical tips (pulse points, layering, storage)
- Seasonal guides: "Best Fragrances for Summer 2024", "Holiday Gift Guide"
- Note profiles: deep dives on popular notes (vanilla, oud, bergamot, sandalwood)
- Fragrance family guides with examples and recommendations from the catalog

### Content Integration Points
- Homepage: educational teaser sections that link to full guides
- Product pages: related educational content based on the product's characteristics
- Quiz results: educational context about the user's scent profile
- Search: when no results found, suggest educational starting points
- Empty states: fill dead-end pages with relevant learning content

### SEO & Content Marketing
- Each educational article should target specific long-tail keywords
- "What does [note] smell like" — high-intent informational queries
- "Best fragrances for [occasion/season/personality]" — commercial-adjacent queries
- Build topical authority in fragrance discovery and education
- Internal linking strategy: educational content ↔ product pages ↔ quiz
