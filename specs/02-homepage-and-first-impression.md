# Homepage & First Impression

## Job to Be Done

A first-time visitor who knows nothing about fragrance should land on the homepage and within 5 seconds understand: (1) this is about discovering fragrances, (2) there's a free quiz that will help them personally, and (3) this feels trustworthy and beautiful enough to explore. The homepage must convert curiosity into quiz starts.

## Current State

- Hero section: static "Discover Your Signature Scent" headline with a single CTA
- Featured Fragrances section: grid of 4 product cards pulled from backend
- No storytelling, no social proof, no educational content
- No visual hook — the hero is text-on-background with no imagery or animation
- No explanation of what makes the quiz valuable or different
- No content below the fold that rewards scrolling
- The page doesn't communicate the brand's point of view on fragrance

## Desired Outcomes

### Hero Section
- The hero should be immersive — a full-viewport experience that communicates "scent" visually (abstract imagery, gradient overlays, particle effects, or editorial photography)
- Primary CTA ("Find Your Scent" or similar) should be unmissable and inviting, not corporate
- Secondary context: a one-liner that addresses the visitor's anxiety ("Not sure where to start? We'll help you find your perfect fragrance — for free.")
- Consider animated or rotating elements that showcase the diversity of fragrance (families, moods, occasions)

### Value Proposition Section
- Immediately below the hero: explain the quiz value prop in 3-4 concise points
- Visual cards or icons: "Tell us about you" → "We analyze 100+ fragrances" → "Get personalized picks"
- This section must make the quiz feel smart and personal, not generic

### Social Proof & Trust
- Testimonials, user count, or quiz completion stats (even if initially simulated for MVP)
- "As featured in" or brand partner logos if available
- User-generated content section or community highlights

### Editorial Content
- A "Fragrance 101" or educational teaser section — position ScentScape as a guide, not just a store
- Trending or seasonal picks with editorial context ("What people are wearing this spring")
- Blog post previews or fragrance stories

### Below-the-Fold Experience
- The page should reward scrolling with progressively deeper content
- Collection highlights with visual storytelling (not just grid cards)
- A "How It Works" section that demystifies fragrance for beginners
- Final CTA section that re-invites quiz participation

### Performance & Loading
- Above-the-fold content must be visually complete within 1.5 seconds
- Images should use blur placeholders or skeleton loading
- Content sections should lazy-load as user scrolls
