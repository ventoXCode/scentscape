# Monetization Strategy & Revenue Architecture

## Job to Be Done

ScentScape must establish revenue paths that align with its core mission: making fragrance accessible. The quiz and educational content remain free — no paywall. Monetization should feel like a natural extension of the value provided, not a gate blocking it. Revenue should grow proportionally with user trust and engagement.

## Current State

- Single revenue stream: direct product sales (B2C e-commerce via Medusa + Stripe)
- Product pricing in seed data: $95-$225 per fragrance
- No affiliate relationships
- No subscription or recurring revenue model
- No premium features or tiers
- No advertising or sponsored content
- No data monetization
- No partnerships or referral programs
- Quiz is free but doesn't connect to any revenue path beyond "browse these products"

## Desired Outcomes

### Primary Revenue: Affiliate & Referral Commerce
- ScentScape should NOT stock or ship fragrances directly (capital-intensive, logistics-heavy)
- Instead: become the trusted recommendation layer that connects users to authorized retailers
- Affiliate partnerships with major fragrance retailers (Sephora, Nordstrom, FragranceNet, etc.)
- Each product page and quiz result should include "Buy at [Retailer]" links with affiliate tracking
- Commission-based revenue: typically 5-15% per referred sale in beauty/fragrance
- This aligns with the mission: users get unbiased recommendations, ScentScape earns when they convert

### Secondary Revenue: Sample & Discovery Boxes
- Partner with sample/decant services to offer "Try Before You Buy" sample kits
- "Your Quiz Results Sampler" — 3-5 samples of recommended fragrances shipped to the user
- This is a lower-commitment entry point ($20-40 vs $100+ for full bottles)
- ScentScape curates, partner fulfills
- Revenue: markup on sample kits + ongoing affiliate revenue when users upgrade to full bottles

### Tertiary Revenue: Premium Experiences
- Free tier (current): quiz, recommendations, browsing, educational content
- Premium tier (future consideration): 
  - Saved scent profiles with history
  - AI-powered conversational fragrance advisor
  - Exclusive editorial content or early access to new fragrance reviews
  - Community features (reviews, ratings, discussions)
  - Monthly "scent briefing" personalized newsletter
- Important: premium features should enhance, never gate the core quiz experience

### Data & Insights (Long-term)
- Aggregate (anonymized) preference data is valuable to fragrance brands
- Trend reports: "What Gen Z is wearing", seasonal preference shifts, emerging note preferences
- Brand partnerships: fragrances can pay for featured placement or sponsored quiz results (clearly labeled)
- Market research services for fragrance houses launching new products

### Revenue Architecture Principles
- The quiz must ALWAYS be free — it's the top of the funnel and the brand differentiator
- Monetization should feel like service, not extraction
- Every revenue path should make the user experience better, not worse
- Trust is the primary asset — never compromise recommendation quality for revenue
- Start with affiliate commerce (lowest friction, highest alignment), add complexity as user base grows
